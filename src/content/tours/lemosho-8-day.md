The 8-day Lemosho Route offers the highest summit success rate of any Kilimanjaro route. With maximum time for acclimatization, this itinerary traverses the pristine western slopes, crosses the entire Shira Plateau, and includes a full rest day at Karanga Camp. The extra days on the mountain mean a more gradual ascent and a deeper wilderness experience.

## Itinerary

### Day 1: Londorossi Gate – Mti Mkubwa Camp

Drive through lush farmland to Londorossi Gate. Trek through untouched montane rainforest along a quiet trail. The forest is dense with ancient trees, moss, and ferns. Arrive at Mti Mkubwa Camp (2,820m), named for the giant fig tree near the campsite.

**Highlights:** Peaceful rainforest trek away from the busier routes.

**Accommodation:** Mti Mkubwa Camp
**Meal Plan:** Lunch, Dinner, Breakfast

### Day 2: Mti Mkubwa Camp – Shira Camp 1

Ascend through the forest, which gradually thins as you gain altitude. The trail opens onto the Shira Plateau with dramatic views. Arrive at Shira Camp 1 (3,500m). Afternoon short walk for acclimatization and sunset photography.

**Highlights:** First views of the vast Shira Plateau and Kilimanjaro's glaciers.

**Accommodation:** Shira Camp 1
**Meal Plan:** Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

### Day 3: Shira Camp 1 – Shira Camp 2

Traverse the rolling Shira Plateau. This high-altitude desert is punctuated by volcanic cones and giant groundsels. Arrive early at Shira Camp 2 (3,850m). Afternoon acclimatization hike to Cathedral Point (3,950m).

**Highlights:** Easy walking day with spectacular plateau scenery and acclimatization.

**Accommodation:** Shira Camp 2
**Meal Plan:** Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

### Day 4: Shira Camp 2 – Lava Tower – Barranco Camp

Ascend to Lava Tower (4,630m), a striking volcanic plug, for lunch and altitude exposure. Descend to the lush Barranco Valley, arriving at Barranco Camp (3,960m). The descent allows excellent acclimatization.

**Highlights:** Climb-high, sleep-low strategy at the iconic Lava Tower.

**Accommodation:** Barranco Camp
**Meal Plan:** Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

### Day 5: Barranco Camp – Karanga Camp

Conquer the Barranco Wall, a steep scramble with spectacular drops. Continue through the Karanga Valley, crossing several ridges. Arrive at Karanga Camp (4,035m) in the early afternoon. Rest and hydrate.

**Highlights:** The thrilling Barranco Wall, one of Kilimanjaro's most famous features.

**Accommodation:** Karanga Camp
**Meal Plan:** Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

### Day 6: Karanga Camp – Acclimatization Day

A full acclimatization day at Karanga. Hike to higher altitudes on the mountain's slopes and descend back to camp. This extra day provides the best possible preparation for the summit. Rest, foot care, and mental preparation.

**Highlights:** Dedicated rest and acclimatization for peak summit success.

**Accommodation:** Karanga Camp
**Meal Plan:** Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

### Day 7: Karanga Camp – Barafu Camp

Ascend through alpine desert to Barafu Camp (4,673m). The landscape is stark, with scree slopes and volcanic rock. Set up camp, prepare summit gear, and eat an early dinner. Rest before the midnight departure.

**Highlights:** Final base camp with views of the summit cone.

**Accommodation:** Barafu Camp
**Meal Plan:** Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

### Day 8: Summit Day – Descent to Mweka

Depart at midnight for the summit. Reach Stella Point (5,756m) at sunrise along the crater rim, then continue to Uhuru Peak (5,895m) for photos and celebration. Descend via the Mweka Route to Mweka Camp (3,100m) for the final night.

**Highlights:** Sunrise at Uhuru Peak, the highest point on the African continent.

**Accommodation:** Mweka Camp
**Meal Plan:** Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
